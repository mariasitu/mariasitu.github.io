Even as a child I knew I wanted to build worlds and tell stories. This path led me to pursue a film career in college and I spent many a long night and day working in different positions within the Art Department.

I've built a jail cell inside a rundown prison, walked alongside 8 feet tall magical birds looking for glowing eggs, and put out bowls to collect water dripping from a lemon tree growing out of a man's stomach.

While in a different medium, this process of storytelling, of taking some intangible thing and making it real felt (still feels) tremendously rewarding.

As designers, we are tasked with creating very specific experiences. We use our special set of problem solving skills to bridge the gaps left by user pain points and unaddressed needs.

I believe that design is a universal language and we use it as a tool to tell stories. It helps bring people together and allows us to share similar feelings and experiences that overcome physical or figurative barriers.
