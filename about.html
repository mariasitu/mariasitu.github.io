ABOUT ME

I used to work on film sets, but now I mostly like to

drink strong coffee and climb rock walls in my free time.

Graduating with a degree in Radio, Film, and Television from UT Austin in 2016, I fully intended to pack up my bags and pursue my dream of being a Production Designer in LA. And then I stumbled into a tech job and now I'm spoiled.

While never intending to work in the tech industry, I discovered that I loved the challenge of working at a small startup like YETI Custom Shop and solving problems in a fast paced setting.

And so I changed paths, building up my skillset in data entry, e-commerce operations, customer service, cross department coordination, and later, project management.

While solving technical and practical problems on a daily basis was engaging and rewarding, there was something missing. I felt an urge to create and design that wasn't being fulfilled. So I did some research about how I could marry my two passions and discovered that creative design + problem solving + tech = user experience design.

And then everything changed.

I spent a year completing Bloc.io's UI/UX Designer Track bootcamp and apprenticeship, learning the fundamentals of user experience and honing my new set of skills in user research, ideation, protyping, user testing, and iteration.

I believe that design is a universal language and we can use it as a tool to solve problems and bring people together by creating user-based empathetic, intentional, and meaningful experiences.

Want to chat? I'm interested in all things related to design, tech, art, and the human experience and how it all intersects.
